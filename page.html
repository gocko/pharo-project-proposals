Pharo GSOC 2019: Ideas List

Welcome to the one-stop-shop for Google Summer of Code 2019 with Pharo Consortium aspiring to provide the ultimate immersive programming experience. Pharo is a pure object-oriented programming language and a powerful environment, focused on simplicity and immediate feedback (think IDE and OS rolled into one).

A glimpse of Pharo

  • Simple & powerful language: No constructors, no types declaration, no interfaces, no primitive types. Yet a powerful and elegant language with a full syntax fitting in one postcard! Pharo is objects and messages all the way down.
  • Live, immersive environment: Immediate feedback at any moment of your development: Developing, testing, debugging. Even in production environments, you will never be stuck in compiling and deploying steps again!
  • Amazing debugging experience: The Pharo environment includes a debugger unlike anything you've seen before. It allows you to step through code, restart the execution of methods, create methods on the fly, and much more!
  • Pharo is yours: Pharo is made by an incredible community, with more than 100 contributors for the last revision of the platform and hundreds of people contributing constantly with frameworks and libraries.

General Information for Students

Hello Pharo enthusiasts ! Thank you for your interest in Pharo Consortium. The pre-requisites for applying include knowledge about object oriented programming, basic idea about Pharo/Smalltalk syntax. The idea list for the possible projects is a result of contributions from developers and users which might not be complete on their own. As a prospective student, you should contact the developer and/or the project maintainer, for further clarifications, discussions and the interest in the project during the application period should be made pretty evident via your Github contributions and interactions with the mentors (EMails, mailing lists, chat channels, etc.). If there is no specific contact given you can ask questions on the general Pharo development list pharo-dev@lists.pharo.org. See the Pharo community page for information on available mailing lists and how to subscribe. Being accepted as a Google Summer of Code student is quite competitive. Accepted students typically have thoroughly researched the technologies of their proposed project and have been in frequent contact with potential mentors. Simply copying and pasting an idea here will not work. On the other hand, creating a completely new idea without first consulting potential mentors is unlikely to work out as well.

Adding a proposal as a mentor

In case you are a Pharo developer and have a compelling idea for this year's Google Summer of Code, we shall be happy to include your proposal to the list. Kindly add your project idea to the list of topics in the following template on github (here at github.com/pharo-project/pharo-project-proposals/blob/master/Topics.st) and send a pull request.

     PharoTopic new
       title: 'name of your project';
       contact: 'email address';
       supervisors: 'mentor(s) names';
       keywords: 'keywords separated by spaces';
       context: 'description of the context of the project';
       goal: 'description of the goal';
       level: 'beginner, or intermediate, or advanced';
       yourself.
